Academic Projects

  1. Development of a Cost- Effective Programmable Syringe Pump.

    The aim was to develop a cost effective programmable syringe pump. The commercially available syringe pumps cost almost 3500$ whereas we have manufactured a simple more efficient syringe pump that costs at most 88$. Tt was calibrated using local syringes by developing a regression equation with R2 value of 0.99 for volume and 1 for flow rate respectively. Most of the components have been 3D printed which reduced the cost and most importantly it made the syringe pump a customizable one. Also open source Arduino code has been developed for its use so the user is free to program it and also change its design according to his necessity.

    Solidworks Design

  2. Protective Cover for Tracheostomy Tube.

    Tracheostomy Tubes are curved tubes that are being used to treat various laryngeal and pharyngeal disorders and diseases. With increased importance and popularity, tracheostomy tubes are widely used, but its construction is not flawless. The prevailing commercial tracheostomy tubes are prone to danger of water, dust and insect infestation. The purpose of this project is to design and develop a protective cover for tracheostomy tube to prevent the probability of causing extreme hazards of the patients by unexpected deposition of water droplet, dust particle and insects in the tracheostomy tube. Through stepwise approach and trial error method for evaluation of design, the final prototype of the protective cover for tracheostomy tube is developed.

    Solidworks Design

Voluntary Projects for COVID-19

  • High-Performance UV Disinfection Cabinet for Masks and Personal Protective Equipment

    To minimize the disaster and casualties caused by the recent COVID-19 pandemic, UV-C disinfection chambers were manufactured with a total of 416 W. to effectively disinfect the COVID-19 virus with UV-C irradiation which has the wavelength in the range of 200-280 nm and is the germicidal range. According to CDC and IUVA, ultraviolet germicidal irradiation of 0.5-1 J/cm2 is 99.9% effective to inactivate influenza and coronaviruses. Due to the high wattage, the developed chamber was capable of achieving the required dose in a short amount of time.
